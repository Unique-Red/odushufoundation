About Odushu Foundation

A registered charity dedicated to the empowerment of the boy child through comprehensive support and community engagement.

Our Mission

To advance the education, health, and welfare of children, particularly boys who are disadvantaged or at risk, through comprehensive support programmes and community engagement.

Our Vision

A society where every boy, regardless of their background or circumstance, has the resources and support they need to become a confident, responsible, and positive pillar for the next generation.

Our Values

Compassion and empathy, Integrity and transparency, Child-centered approach, Empowerment and Growth.

What We Do

Our initiatives are designed to create multiple avenues for holistic development and lasting impact.

Educational Support

Providing essential resources and facilities to ensure no child is left behind in their academic journey.

Mental Health Services

Offering counseling, safe spaces, and therapy to help boys navigate psychological challenges.

Mentorship Programmes

Connecting boys with role models who provide guidance, wisdom, and direction for personal growth.

Skills Training

Vocational training and workshops to equip boys with practical skills for future employability.

Health & Wellness

Healthcare check-ups, nutritional guidance, and sports activities for physical well-being.

Leadership Development

Programmes designed to instill confidence, accountability, and the ability to inspire others.

Our Constitution

The governing principles that guide our work and ensure accountability.

Constitution of Odushu Foundation

1

Name

The organization shall be known, legally and formally, as the Odushu Foundation.

2

Objectives

To advance the education, health, and welfare of children, with a particular focus on boys, through:

  • Providing education and skills training.
  • Supporting mental health initiatives.
  • Offering mentorship programmes.
3

Powers

To do anything necessary to achieve its objectives, including but not limited to fundraising, entering into partnerships, and managing funds.

4

Membership

Membership is open to any individual who supports the charity's objectives and wishes to contribute to its mission.

5

Trustees

The charity shall be managed by a board of at least 3 trustees, elected annually, who oversee the charity's strategy and finances.

6

Meetings

The charity shall hold an Annual General Meeting (AGM) and other meetings as necessary to conduct business.

7

Finance

All funds shall be managed responsibly and applied solely to the objectives of the charity. No profits shall be distributed to members.

8

Amendments

Changes to this constitution require a 2/3 majority vote at a properly constituted meeting of members.

9

Dissolution

In the event of dissolution, any remaining assets after the payment of debts and liabilities shall be distributed to similar charitable organizations.
